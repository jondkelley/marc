Asylum Lawyer – Kalamazoo Immigration Attorney Marc Asch

Protecting Those Seeking Refuge from Persecution

For some immigrants, the United States is more than just a place of opportunity—it is a refuge from persecution. At the Law Office of Marc Asch in Kalamazoo, Michigan, we represent clients in the critical and often life-saving process of applying for asylum.

Our clients include individuals fleeing:

Types of Persecution We Handle

We represent individuals fleeing various forms of persecution recognized under U.S. asylum law.

Political Persecution

Political persecution or unstable political climates

Identity-Based Threats

Threats or harm due to race, religion, or nationality

Social Group Targeting

Targeting based on membership in a particular social group

Other Forms

Other forms of persecution recognized under U.S. asylum law

Asylum as a Defense Against Deportation

Many people facing deportation after being detained inside the U.S. or at the border request asylum as a defense.

These cases are complex, and eligibility for asylum is never guaranteed. An experienced immigration attorney can make the difference between protection in the U.S. and return to dangerous conditions.

Complex Cases

Asylum cases involve intricate legal requirements and evidence

Legal Expertise

Experienced attorneys can make the critical difference

The stakes are high—your safety and future depend on proper legal representation.

How the Asylum Process Works

When you meet with Kalamazoo asylum lawyer Marc Asch, we will:

Eligibility Evaluation

Evaluate your eligibility for asylum

Application Preparation

Prepare and file an affirmative asylum application with the appropriate asylum office if no removal proceedings are pending

Court Representation

Represent you before an immigration judge if your asylum request is filed as part of removal proceedings

Asylum and Refugee Status Benefits

If your asylum application is granted, you gain more than just safety.

You may also:

Family Reunification

Petition for your spouse and children to join you in the United States

Permanent Residence

Apply for lawful permanent residence (a green card) after one year in asylum status
