Immigration Appeals & Federal Court Litigation – Kalamazoo Immigration Lawyer Marc Asch

Challenging Unjust Decisions & Protecting Your Rights

Securing lawful permanent residency or U.S. citizenship is a critical milestone for any immigrant. Unfortunately, mistakes in the legal process—or unfavorable decisions by immigration judges—can stand in the way. At the Law Office of Marc Asch in Kalamazoo, Michigan, we represent clients in immigration appeals and federal court litigation to challenge unjust decisions and protect their rights.

When to Appeal an Immigration Decision

If you have been denied relief by an immigration judge after going through the court process, you may be able to appeal the decision.

We examine cases for:

Errors in Applying Immigration Law

Mistakes in legal interpretation or application

Incorrect Interpretation of Facts

Misunderstanding or misapplication of evidence

Misuse of Judicial Discretion

Abuse or improper use of judicial authority

Immigration laws and policies change frequently—what was prohibited in the past may be permitted on appeal. In addition, changes in your personal circumstances (such as marriage, new qualifying relatives, or humanitarian factors) may strengthen your case for reconsideration.

Federal Court Immigration Litigation

If your immigration case—such as a citizenship application or green card petition—is stuck in USCIS processing delays far beyond normal timelines, we can file a federal court lawsuit (writ of mandamus) to compel USCIS to make a decision.

This legal action can be essential when delays threaten your ability to work, travel, or reunite with family members.

Writ of Mandamus

Federal court action to compel USCIS decision-making

Processing Delays

Addressing unreasonable delays in case processing

Work Authorization

Protecting your ability to work legally

Travel Rights

Maintaining freedom to travel when needed

Family Reunification

Ensuring timely family reunification
