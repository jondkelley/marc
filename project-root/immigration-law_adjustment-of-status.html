Adjustment of Status

Your Path to Permanent Residency While Living in the United States

For individuals already living in the United States, it is often possible to obtain a green card by applying for an Adjustment of Status (AOS) based on family relationships, employment, investment, asylum status, or other qualifying categories.

You May Qualify for Adjustment of Status If You:

Have an Approved Immigrant Petition

Your family member or employer has successfully filed and received approval for an immigrant petition on your behalf.

Special Immigrant Juvenile Petition

You are applying under a "special immigrant juvenile petition" or "special immigrant military petition."

Derivative Spouse or Child

You are a derivative spouse or child of the principal AOS applicant, with both applications filed at the same time.

Derivative of Visa Holder

You are a derivative spouse or child of the principal immigrant visa holder.

Important Considerations

Important: Adjustment of Status is not a simple or automatic process. These criteria are only a starting point—there are numerous other requirements.

For example, you generally cannot qualify if you entered the United States without a valid visa or if you entered as a nonimmigrant crew member. Many factors can complicate an AOS application, including incomplete documentation, prior immigration violations, or certain criminal convictions.

Common Disqualifying Factors:

  • Entered without a valid visa
  • Nonimmigrant crew member status
  • Prior immigration violations
  • Certain criminal convictions

Application Complications:

  • Incomplete documentation
  • Missing required forms
  • Insufficient evidence
  • Timing issues
