Immigration Interview Preparation – Kalamazoo Immigration Lawyer Marc Asch

Preparing for Success in Your Immigration Journey

An immigration interview can be one of the most high-stakes moments in your journey to live and work in the United States. Whether it's for a green card, citizenship, asylum, or another immigration benefit, the outcome of your interview can determine your future.

At the Law Office of Marc Asch in Kalamazoo, Michigan, we help clients prepare for every type of immigration interview—ensuring they know what to expect, how to present themselves, and how to answer questions confidently and truthfully.

Preparing You for Your Immigration Interview

With extensive experience in family-based immigration, citizenship/naturalization, asylum, and consular processing, we provide personalized interview preparation that includes:

Application Review

Reviewing your entire immigration application packet to ensure all information is accurate and up to date

Making Corrections

Making necessary corrections at the interview, if applicable

Interview Format

Explaining the format of your interview and the types of questions you are likely to be asked

Practice Responses

Helping you practice clear, consistent, and truthful responses—especially for high-scrutiny cases like marriage-based green cards, where spouses may be interviewed separately and answers compared

Our preparation process is designed to increase your confidence, reduce anxiety, and help you avoid mistakes that could hurt your case.

Even when we cannot be physically present at your interview, we make sure you walk in fully prepared to present your case in the best light possible.

Essential Immigration Interview Tips

While every case is unique, there are some universal rules for a successful immigration interview:

Dress Professionally

Show you are serious about your application

Be Respectful

Courtesy toward immigration officers can influence how your case is handled

Treat It Like a Job Interview

Sit up straight, avoid gum, and maintain eye contact

Prepare Thoroughly

This may be the most important interview of your life; take it seriously

Remember: It's a Request

Approach the process with humility and professionalism
