Who Can Qualify for a Family Immigration Visa?

If you are a United States citizen, you may petition for your:

  • Spouse
  • Parents
  • Minor children

Over time, U.S. citizens may also sponsor:

  • Adult children (married or unmarried)
  • Brothers and sisters

If you are a lawful permanent resident (green card holder), you may petition for your:

  • Spouse
  • Unmarried adult and minor children
Important: In all cases, the sponsoring petitioner or a qualified co-sponsor must meet USCIS income requirements. Working with an experienced immigration lawyer in Kalamazoo can significantly improve your chances of avoiding costly delays or denials.

Legal Guidance for Complex Immigration Situations

Our office can file your petition directly with U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) or coordinate with the appropriate U.S. consulate to arrange interviews abroad. If a family member entered the United States without inspection or overstayed their visa, they may need to return to their home country for consular processing. Past removal orders or criminal convictions can make a case more difficult, but our firm works to find solutions and overcome legal obstacles wherever possible.

Understanding Visa Backlogs

Family-based immigration categories often have annual quotas. Once a category's limit is reached for the year, your family member may face an extended wait before a visa becomes available. We will help you navigate the visa bulletin and plan accordingly to reduce unnecessary delays.

Immigration Relief Under the Violence Against Women Act (VAWA)

The Violence Against Women Act provides certain immigration protections for non-citizens who have experienced abuse. Under VAWA, eligible individuals may apply for permanent residency through self-petitioning or cancellation of removal without the involvement of the abusive family member. Our office can assess your eligibility and guide you through this sensitive and confidential process.
