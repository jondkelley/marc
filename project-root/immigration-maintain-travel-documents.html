Maintaining Immigration Status & Travel Documents – Kalamazoo Immigration Lawyer Marc Asch

Protecting Your Legal Status & Ensuring Safe Travel

Once you have legal immigration status in the United States, it is critical to protect it. Falling out of status can lead to serious immigration consequences, including removal (deportation), loss of your ability to return to the U.S., and difficulties in adjusting your status to lawful permanent residence.

If you plan to travel outside the U.S., you must also have the correct travel documents to ensure you can reenter without issues. At the Law Office of Marc Asch in Kalamazoo, Michigan, we advise clients on maintaining lawful status and securing the proper travel documentation to avoid costly mistakes.

Maintaining Your Immigration Status

Falling out of status can:

Deportation Risk

Put you at risk for deportation or removal proceedings

Re-entry Issues

Make it impossible to return to the U.S. after international travel

Application Complications

Complicate or prevent your green card or adjustment of status application

Common ways people lose status include:

Student Visa Violations

Such as working without proper authorization

Overstaying a Visa

Remaining in the U.S. beyond the authorized period

Green Card Abandonment

Spending more than six months outside the U.S. can trigger a presumption of abandonment; being outside for more than a year may cause you to lose permanent resident status

We explain your legal obligations, monitor key deadlines, and guide you in staying compliant with U.S. immigration laws.

Travel Documents for Immigrants

Maintaining your status is one of the many reasons to have the correct travel documents before leaving the United States. We assist with:

Advance Parole

Required if you have a pending green card application and plan to travel abroad before it is approved

Re-entry Permit

For permanent residents traveling outside the U.S. for more than six months but less than two years

Refugee Travel Document

Allows refugees and asylees to travel internationally and return to the U.S.

We also help ensure your green card is renewed on time and your foreign passport is valid before travel.
