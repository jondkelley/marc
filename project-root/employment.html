Practice Areas – Kalamazoo Employment Lawyer Marc Asch

Protecting Workplace Rights in Kalamazoo and Across Michigan

When you go to work, you expect fair treatment and compliance with Michigan and federal employment laws. Unfortunately, employers do not always uphold these obligations. Some engage in illegal discrimination, making decisions based on a worker's identity instead of their qualifications or performance. Others fail to comply with wage and hour laws, deny employees their entitled leave, or ignore legal protections for public-sector workers.

Marc Asch, an experienced Kalamazoo employment attorney, represents employees in discrimination cases, wage disputes, leave rights violations, and other workplace legal matters.

Employment Discrimination

Employment discrimination occurs when an employer treats an employee differently because of their membership in a protected class. This can include refusal to hire, failure to promote, unequal pay, or workplace harassment.

Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964

Prohibits discrimination based on race, color, national origin, religion, and sex.

Michigan Elliott-Larsen Civil Rights Act

Extends protections under Michigan law, applying to employers with one or more employees.

Because filing deadlines are short, it is critical to consult an experienced Kalamazoo employment discrimination lawyer as soon as possible.

Under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and Michigan law, most employees are entitled to minimum wage and overtime pay. Marc Asch advises workers on whether they have claims for unpaid wages and represents them in recovery actions.

Leave Rights

Leave disputes can involve:

FMLA

Federal law granting eligible employees up to 12 weeks of unpaid leave for qualifying medical and family reasons

Michigan Leave Rights

Including maternity and paternity leave

Military Leave

Under the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA)

Employers may not retaliate against workers for exercising their leave rights. If you believe your leave rights have been violated, a Kalamazoo employment law attorney can evaluate your case.

Government Employee Protections

Public employees have unique legal protections against wrongful termination, retaliation, and discrimination.

MSPB Appeals

Federal workers may challenge certain adverse employment actions before the Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB), which has authority over removals, demotions, suspensions, and furloughs.

Whistleblower Protections

Marc Asch advises public employees on MSPB appeals, whistleblower protections, and due process violations.
