Temporary Protected Status (TPS) – Kalamazoo Immigration Attorney Marc Asch

Protection from Deportation During Crisis

The United States offers Temporary Protected Status (TPS) to foreign nationals from certain countries facing armed conflict, environmental disasters, or other extraordinary conditions. TPS provides temporary protection from deportation for individuals who would face serious danger if forced to return home.

At the Law Office of Marc Asch in Kalamazoo, Michigan, we help TPS applicants understand eligibility requirements, file their applications accurately, and maintain their status for as long as it remains in effect.

Who Qualifies for Temporary Protected Status?

The list of TPS-designated countries changes as political and environmental conditions evolve.

Recent designations have included:

Middle East & Africa

  • Syria
  • Sudan
  • South Sudan
  • Somalia
  • Yemen

Americas & Asia

  • Nicaragua
  • El Salvador
  • Honduras
  • Haiti
  • Nepal
Each country has its own estimated number of TPS beneficiaries—ranging from hundreds to hundreds of thousands.

Key Features of TPS

Protection Benefits

  • Cannot be removed from the United States
  • May protect from deportation or removal proceedings

Work & Travel

  • May be eligible for work authorization
  • I-512 advance parole for temporary travel abroad

Important Limitations

  • TPS does not lead directly to a green card or U.S. citizenship
  • When TPS ends, you return to the immigration status you had before TPS (unless that status has changed)
