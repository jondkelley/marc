Your Path to Lawful Permanent Residency in the United States

A green card is your official proof of lawful permanent residency in the United States. There are several pathways to obtaining a green card, and at the Law Office of Marc Asch, based in Kalamazoo, Michigan, we assist clients through all avenues of the green card process.

Pathways to Obtaining a Green Card

Family Sponsorship

By a U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident, including:

  • Spouse
  • Parents
  • Children
  • Siblings

Employment-Based Sponsorship

For individuals with:

  • Job offers from U.S. employers
  • Specialized skills
  • Advanced degrees

Refugee or Asylee Status

For those granted protection in the United States

Diversity Visa Program

The "green card lottery" for eligible countries

Special Categories

For qualifying family situations and specialized cases

Responsibilities of Green Card Holders

Important: Lawful permanent residents must follow certain requirements. The most important is carrying your green card at all times.

Under the Immigration and Nationality Act, failure to carry your green card can result in a fine of up to $100 and even imprisonment for up to 30 days.

What You Must Do:

  • Carry your green card at all times
  • Notify USCIS of address changes
  • File income tax returns
  • Maintain continuous residence

What You Can Do:

  • Live and work anywhere in the U.S.
  • Apply for U.S. citizenship after 5 years
  • Sponsor family members
  • Travel internationally

Avoid Green Card Scams

Warning: Unfortunately, green card scams are common. Fraudsters may take your money and falsely promise to file paperwork or guarantee a win in the green card lottery.

If an offer sounds too good to be true, it probably is. Work only with experienced, licensed immigration attorneys who understand U.S. immigration law and can protect your rights.

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • Guaranteed approval promises
  • Requests for payment before services
  • Pressure to act immediately
  • Unlicensed "consultants"

How to Protect Yourself:

  • Verify attorney credentials
  • Get everything in writing
  • Never pay cash
  • Trust your instincts
