Overcoming Obstacles to a Spouse or Fiancé(e) Visa

The fiancé(e) visa process typically involves five steps:

1

File USCIS Petition

Filing a petition with U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) from within the U.S.

2

Case Transfer

Upon approval, your case is sent to the U.S. consulate in your fiancé(e)'s country.

3

Documentation

Your fiancé(e) completes and submits all required documentation to the consulate.

4

Medical Examination

Your fiancé(e) undergoes a medical examination by an approved physician.

5

Consular Interview

Your fiancé(e) attends a consular interview, after which the visa may be granted.

Common Challenges in the K-1 Visa Process

While the process sounds straightforward, many applications are denied each year for preventable reasons.

Common causes of denial include:

Incomplete Documentation

Missing or incomplete forms and supporting evidence

Minor Factual Mistakes

Small errors that can lead to application rejection

Misunderstanding Requirements

Lack of clarity about what's needed

Additional Complications

In some cases, an uncooperative consular officer can further complicate matters, forcing couples to restart the process. This is why having experienced legal representation is crucial.
